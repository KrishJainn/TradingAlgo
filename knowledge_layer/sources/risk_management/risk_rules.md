# Risk Management Rules

## Golden Rules

1. **Never risk more than 2% per trade** - This is non-negotiable
2. **Maximum daily drawdown: 6%** - Stop trading if exceeded
3. **Maximum weekly drawdown: 10%** - Reduce size if approaching
4. **Correlation risk** - Avoid multiple positions in correlated assets

## Position Sizing by Player Type

### Aggressive Player
- Normal risk: 1.5-2% per trade
- High volatility: 1% per trade
- Maximum positions: 5-7 simultaneous
- Stop loss: 2-3 ATR

### Conservative Player
- Normal risk: 0.75-1% per trade
- High volatility: 0.5% per trade
- Maximum positions: 3-4 simultaneous
- Stop loss: 1.5-2 ATR

### Balanced Player
- Normal risk: 1-1.5% per trade
- High volatility: 0.75% per trade
- Maximum positions: 4-5 simultaneous
- Stop loss: 2 ATR

### Momentum Player
- Normal risk: 1.25-1.75% per trade
- High volatility: 0.75% per trade
- Maximum positions: 4-6 simultaneous
- Stop loss: 2.5 ATR with trailing

### Volatility Breakout Player
- Normal risk: 1-1.5% per trade
- High volatility: Actually optimal conditions
- Maximum positions: 3-4 simultaneous
- Stop loss: Based on breakout range

## Drawdown Recovery

### After 5% Drawdown
- Reduce position sizes by 25%
- Increase entry threshold by 0.05
- Review recent losing trades

### After 10% Drawdown
- Reduce position sizes by 50%
- Increase entry threshold by 0.10
- Consider paper trading until recovery

### After 15% Drawdown
- Stop live trading
- Full strategy review required
- Paper trade for minimum 2 weeks

## Stop Loss Guidelines

### Fixed Stops
- Use in ranging/choppy markets
- Based on recent support/resistance
- Typically 1.5-2.5 ATR

### Trailing Stops
- Use in trending markets
- Trail by 2-3 ATR in strong trends
- Lock in profits on extended moves

### Time-Based Stops
- Exit if trade doesn't move in 5-10 bars
- Prevents capital being tied up
- Frees capital for better opportunities

## Risk-Adjusted Returns

Always evaluate performance on risk-adjusted basis:
- **Sharpe Ratio** > 1.0 is acceptable, > 2.0 is excellent
- **Sortino Ratio** focuses on downside volatility
- **Maximum Drawdown** should be contained
- **Recovery Factor** = Total Profit / Max Drawdown

## Emergency Protocols

### Flash Crash
- All stops should be market orders
- Have circuit breakers in place
- Consider using options for protection

### System Failure
- Know how to manually close positions
- Have backup broker access
- Document all emergency procedures
